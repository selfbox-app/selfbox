# Selfbox — Impeccable Design Guide

## Design Context

### Users
Selfbox serves **indie developers and startup founders** deploying on Railway. They're technically fluent, taste-aware, and time-constrained. They use Selfbox to self-host file storage (as a Dropbox/Drive alternative) for their products or teams — either as end-users of their own apps, or as operators managing workspaces. Their context is usually a browser tab alongside Linear, GitHub, Railway dashboards, and a terminal. They expect keyboard-first ergonomics, fast interactions, and no friction.

### Brand Personality
**Friendly & approachable × Bold & opinionated.** Warm without being cute. Confident without being corporate. Every interaction should feel human and considered, but the product takes strong stances on defaults and UX — we don't hedge. Voice is direct, specific, occasionally witty. No "empowering your workflow" marketing-speak. No "🚀 Let's get started!" exuberance.

The emotions the interface should evoke: **trust** (it's safe to put your files here), **control** (you own this, not a cloud vendor), **craft** (someone clearly cared about every detail), and **momentum** (it's fast and gets out of your way).

### Aesthetic Direction

**References (what we ARE):**
- **Raycast** — keyboard-first, command-palette DNA, crisp Mac-native feel, subtle motion, dark/light parity done right
- **Autosend** — editorial, confident typography, warm accent color used sparingly, generous whitespace, brand shines through without shouting

**Anti-references (what we are NOT):**
- **Generic SaaS dashboards** — no gradient buttons, no stock Lucide icon grids, no "AI glass card" aesthetic
- **Consumer/playful** — no rounded-everything, no pastel mascots, no cutesy micro-copy ("Oopsie!")
- **Enterprise/corporate** — no heavy navbars, no stock photos of diverse teams, no bloated forms with 12 required fields
- **Classic Dropbox/Drive** — we're the modern alternative, not a skin of the incumbent. Should feel 2026, not 2014.

**Theme & Motion:**
- **Light mode default**, dark mode available and equally considered (not an afterthought)
- **Rich, purposeful motion** on marketing pages — scroll reveals, gradient shifts, hero motion — but never gratuitous
- **Subtle motion** in the product UI — hover, focus, state transitions. Nothing delays the user.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` everywhere

**Color:**
- Primary: warm orange `oklch(0.65 0.19 41)` — used sparingly for single points of focus (CTAs, active nav, key accents)
- Everything else: neutral grays in oklch space — white background, dark foreground, subtle borders
- No secondary brand colors. If we add color, it's for semantic meaning (success, destructive, warning) — not decoration.

**Typography:**
- **Geist Sans** for UI and body — modern, technical, excellent at small sizes
- **Geist Mono** for code, file paths, technical details, keyboard shortcuts, labels
- **IBM Plex Serif** for occasional editorial moments (hero quotes, long-form, docs)
- Tight letter-spacing on display sizes (`-0.03em`), weight 500–600 for headings (never 800 heavy)
- Fluid clamps for marketing type — responsive without breakpoint jumps

**Layout:**
- **12-column marketing grid** with fluid gaps (`clamp(1rem, 2.5vw, 2rem)`)
- Generous whitespace — content breathes, nothing feels crammed
- Max content width 80rem — wide but not sprawling
- Dashboard UI: tight information density where it matters (file lists), generous where it doesn't (onboarding, empty states)

### Design Principles

1. **Defaults are opinionated, customization is earned.** The out-of-box experience should feel thoughtful. Advanced settings exist but don't clutter the happy path.

2. **Keyboard first, mouse second.** Every frequent action has a shortcut. Command palette ergonomics. Tab order always makes sense. Focus rings are visible and considered.

3. **Motion with intent.** Animation communicates state, not decoration. Hero pages can be ambitious; dashboards stay out of the way. Always respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

4. **Typography carries the brand.** We don't need illustrations, gradients, or glass morphism. Clean type + one warm accent + considered spacing = the entire visual system.

5. **Own the details.** File icons, empty states, toast copy, error messages, keyboard hints — every surface is an opportunity to show craft. No TODO strings, no Lorem ipsum, no "Something went wrong."

6. **Distinct from the incumbents.** We're not a Dropbox reskin or a Drive clone. Selfbox should feel like a product built in 2026 for people who've outgrown those tools. Modern primitives, modern defaults, modern aesthetic.
